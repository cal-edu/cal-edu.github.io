A body executing linear simple harmonic motion has a velocity of 7.0cm/s when its displacement is 2.0cm, and a velocty of 2.0cm/s when its displacement is 7.0cm. What is the square of the amplitude of oscillation? We use the formula: \[x = A cos(\omega t)\] \[v_x = - A \omega sin(\omega t)\]

The relationship between \(x\) and \(v_x\) \(v_x = \omega\sqrt{A^2 - x^2}\)
